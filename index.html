Mortality Due to Tuberculosis:
A Comparative Analysis by Country

This chart illustrates the trends in mortality rates due to tuberculosis (excluding cases involving HIV) over recent years, from 2015 to 2021. Tuberculosis (TB) is a major global health concern, especially in regions with high population densities and limited healthcare infrastructure. This chart aims to provide a comparative view of TB-related deaths across different countries, highlighting key patterns and disparities in TB mortality rates.

Mortality Cases of Tuberculosis in
China & India


This chart provides a comparative view of mortality cases due to tuberculosis in China and India over a recent period, from 2015 to 2021. Tuberculosis (TB) remains a significant public health challenge, particularly in countries with large populations and varying levels of healthcare access.

China: Confirmed Cases of RR-/MDR-TB

This chart displays the number of confirmed cases of Rifampicin-Resistant (RR) and Multi-Drug Resistant Tuberculosis (MDR-TB) in China over recent years. RR-TB and MDR-TB represent significant challenges in the treatment of tuberculosis, as they are resistant to one or more of the primary TB drugs. The data presented here helps to highlight trends in drug resistance, which is crucial for assessing the effectiveness of current treatment programs and for planning future public health interventions. The dropdown allows for a comparison of this data between China and other countries, offering insight into global TB drug resistance trends.

Tuberculosis Treatment Success Rate
China vs. India


This chart displays the success rates of tuberculosis (TB) treatment in China and India over recent years, from 2015 to 2021. The treatment success rate is a critical metric that reflects the effectiveness of healthcare systems in managing and curing TB cases. This comparison provides insights into how well each country is addressing TB treatment and reflects their healthcare systems' robustness and resource allocation toward TB control.

Global TB Map Based on OECD

This map presents the global distribution of tuberculosis (TB) cases based on data from the OECD. The map highlights regions and countries by the prevalence of TB, using color coding to indicate different rates of occurrence. This visualization aims to provide an overview of how TB is distributed worldwide, with a focus on understanding regional differences in TB incidence. This information can help identify areas that require more attention for TB prevention and treatment initiatives and facilitate a better understanding of the global burden of the disease.
